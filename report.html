01 Overview

Can consumer wearables predict metabolic disease risk without blood tests?

Insulin resistance, quantified by HOMA-IR, is the primary biomarker for Type 2 diabetes risk. Measuring it requires fasting blood draws — expensive, invasive, and inaccessible at population scale. We investigated whether consumer wearable sensors (Fitbit) can approximate HOMA-IR from passively collected health metrics.

Using the WEAR-ME dataset (1,078 participants), we applied autonomous ML hill-climbing — an AI coding agent that iterates through model configurations unsupervised, inspired by Andrej Karpathy's autoresearch.

💡

Key finding: Wearable data alone explains 26.4% of HOMA-IR variance — enough for population-level screening. Adding blood biomarkers reaches 55.4%, capturing ~90% of the dataset's theoretical ceiling (R²≈0.614).

Evaluation Protocol

All results use 5×5 repeated stratified K-Fold cross-validation (25 splits). Every prediction is out-of-fold. No data leakage — the same protocol used across all 28 prior manual experiments.

Model Stack

🌲 XGBoost

Depth 3, 3500 estimators, lr=0.003. Extreme regularization: gamma=100, alpha=5, lambda=20. sqrt(y) sample weighting.

🍃 LightGBM

12 leaves, 3500 estimators, lr=0.003. Leaf-wise growth on raw features. Dominates the blend at 72% weight.

📐 Lasso (L1)

alpha=0.003 on PowerTransformer features. Pure L1 beats ElasticNet — sparse selection more effective than ridge shrinkage.

🔀 Optimized Blend

Grid search over weights + prediction clipping to 0.5th–99.75th percentile. Final: LGB 72% + Lasso 28%.

05 Key Discoveries

What the agent learned from 99 experiments — insights a human researcher missed over 28 manual iterations.

Extreme Regularization (+0.020 R²)

XGBoost gamma=100 — far beyond typical ranges. With only 1,078 samples, aggressive regularization is critical.

Prediction Clipping (+0.004 R²)

Clipping OOF predictions to the 0.5th–99.75th percentile of training targets controls heavy-tail outliers.

Lasso > ElasticNet (+0.001 R²)

Pure L1 regularization outperforms ElasticNet — sparse feature selection is more effective for this data.

PowerTransformer (+0.001 R²)

Yeo-Johnson power transform on inputs before Lasso — the final breakthrough that set the new record.

LightGBM Dominates Blends

LGB with 12 leaves gets 72% blend weight despite XGBoost having higher single-model R². Leaf-wise growth provides complementary predictions.

69% of Ideas Fail

CatBoost, SVR, KNN, neural nets, stacking, target decomposition, feature selection, and multi-seed ensembles all failed.

06 Clinical Implications

What does R²=0.264 from wearables mean for healthcare?

🏥

Consumer wearable data — resting heart rate, HRV, steps, sleep, and activity — combined with basic demographics (age, BMI, sex) can explain 26.4% of variance in insulin resistance, without any blood tests. This is sufficient for population-level risk stratification and early screening.

Information Decomposition: What Predicts HOMA-IR?
Incremental R² contribution by adding each data source

The gap between wearables-only (0.264) and all features (0.554) is primarily driven by fasting glucose — which is part of the HOMA-IR formula by definition. The remaining unexplained variance (R²=0.614 theoretical max) likely reflects measurement noise, genetic factors, and day-to-day biological variability.
